Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, was a prominent leader and a key figure in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He is best known for his philosophy of nonviolent resistance, which he called "Satyagraha."

Gandhi's life was dedicated to achieving political and social change through nonviolent means. He played a crucial role in India's struggle for independence, leading campaigns of nonviolent civil disobedience that inspired people around the world.

Throughout his life, Gandhi advocated for principles such as truth, nonviolence, and self-discipline. His commitment to these values earned him the title "Mahatma," meaning "great soul." Gandhi's teachings continue to influence movements for civil rights and freedom across the globe.
